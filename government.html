iPrepare Business

Development is often stalled, reduced or completely lost when a disaster occurs. When disasters regularly happen,a country’s economy can be in a constant state of build-back what was previously there rather than expanding, improving and developing further. This lack of devtelopment causes greater competition for a reduced amount of assets, creating further conflict and poverty. It is essential that future development is able to not only withstand future hazards but also bounce back better when a disaster does occur.

Inversely, when development is carried out in a manner, which does not consider the current disaster risks or the changing climate risk, the likelihood of a disaster increases.

In the last ten years, the Hyogo Framework for Action 2005-2015 (HFA) has been implemented globally in an attempt to reduce the impact of disasters. There has been some great progress with a significant reduction in the number of lives lost due to disaster. However, economic losses have stayed high and in some countries increased. To ensure that development is sustainable, development, under the new Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015-2030, needs to be governed in a manner which builds resilience to future disaster and climate risks.

ADPC, through its Mainstreaming DRR into Development (MDRD) program, have worked with National Disaster Management Organizations (NDMOs) across Asia to integrate disaster and climate risk considerations into development for over 10 years (2005-2015). The program is developed and implemented by country members of the Regional Consultative Committee on Disaster Management (RCC) in partnership with national sectoral agencies.
